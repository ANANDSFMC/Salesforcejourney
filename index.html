Personal Note
A Letter to Salesforce

Nine years ago, I walked into Salesforce as a Technical Support Engineer, armed with curiosity, a love for solving hard problems, and a genuine belief that great support could change the relationship between a company and its customers.

What I didn't expect was how much this journey would change me — how it would stretch my thinking, challenge my assumptions, and connect me with some of the most brilliant, caring, and driven people I've ever met.

From resolving my first Sev1 case alone at midnight, to leading a team of 8 engineers across EMEA's most strategic accounts — every step has been a privilege. The $1.72M renewals, the 4.85 CSAT, the Red Account recoveries — those numbers represent real people, real businesses, and real trust placed in us.

As I look forward to the decade mark in July 2027, I carry a simple belief: the best support leader is one who makes themselves unnecessary — by building a team so capable, so confident, and so customer-obsessed that excellence becomes the baseline, not the exception.

Thank you, Salesforce. For nine extraordinary years. For the growth, the opportunity, and the chance to do work that matters.

— Anand Kumar B

Manager, Technical Support Engineering · July 2026
